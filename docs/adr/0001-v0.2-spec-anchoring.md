## Problem

`pilot-v0.1` (tag `9a0fc76`, finalised by `pilot-v0.1.x` doc-fix
chain through `94b102a`) shipped against breakout-note v0.2 (commit
`3b7b18af`, see [`docs/conventions.md`](../conventions.md) §"Pilot-spec pin").
Three follow-on additions are now in scope for `pilot-v0.2`:

1. **Rayleigh-number convection gate** — flag cells where buoyancy-driven
   convection would dominate diffusion / sedimentation under
   experimentally plausible vertical temperature gradients.
2. **Hydrodynamic-vs-material radius split** — separate the radius
   that sets buoyant mass (`r_material`, via `ρ_diamond`) from the
   radius that sets drag and diffusion (`r_hydro = r_material +
   δ_shell`), with `δ_shell` accounting for surface coatings,
   solvation shells, or functionalisation.
3. **Polydispersity post-processing** — convolve the v0.1 §5
   sharp-radius classification against a log-normal `P(r | r̄, σ_geom)`
   to produce probabilistic regime labels for realistic experimental
   ensembles.

(Items 4 and 5 from the EC list — aggregation pre-screen and
capsule-geometry port — are out of scope for `v0.2` and live as
separate sibling tracks.)

The Guardian gate question: **does shipping these three additions
require a breakout-note v0.3 spec drift first, or can they extend
v0.1 within the existing envelope?**

## Options considered

### Option A — Block on breakout-note v0.3

`pilot-v0.2` opens only after the breakout note formally absorbs the
three additions: §3 grows a `δ_shell` parameter, §5 grows a `σ_geom`
axis, §5.1 adds a convection footnote. Implementation tracks the
spec.

- **Pros.** Spec authority is preserved; the §5 axes stay
  one-to-one with the implementation grid; no audit-gap pin
  ambiguity.
- **Cons.** Adds 4–6 weeks of upstream blocking on a numerical
  pilot whose scientific yield is largely a function of *running*
  the additions, not specifying them. A `σ_geom` §5 axis would
  5–10× the cache walk wall time without a corresponding scientific
  return at v0.2.

### Option B — Extend v0.1 with audit-gap footnotes upstream

`pilot-v0.2` extends v0.1 within the existing breakout-note envelope:
the three additions land as forward-compatible code paths whose
defaults reproduce v0.1 arithmetic to machine precision. The
breakout note grows three explicit *footnote-level* audit-gap
entries in §5 (matching the Phase-2.5 / Phase-5 audit-gap-pin
convention), to be promoted to first-class §5 axes if and when
experimental data motivate the promotion.

- **Pros.** v0.2 ships in 1.5–2 weeks at v0.1 working tempo. v0.1's
  cache, deliverables, and tag remain valid — `δ_shell = 0,
  ΔT_assumed = 0, σ_geom → 0` is the v0.1 surface, bit-for-bit. The
  audit-gap-pin convention already exists in this repo (§5 t_obs
  values, §5 5th depth value); promoting three more to that
  category is a no-cost extension.
- **Cons.** The breakout note carries footnote-level audit gaps
  rather than first-class spec sections. A reader who reads only
  §3 / §5 of the breakout note may not realise `δ_shell` and
  `σ_geom` are implementation-side knobs.

### Option C — Hybrid (formal spec for one or two, footnote for the rest)

Mixed: convection becomes a §5.1 normative inclusion (it changes
*regime classification*, not just numerical output), while
`δ_shell` and `σ_geom` stay footnote-level audit gaps.

- **Pros.** Convection has stronger physical case for being
  spec-level: it can flip a cell out of the §5.1 classification
  envelope entirely. It belongs in the spec.
- **Cons.** Now the v0.3 breakout-note revision is on the critical
  path, but only for one of the three additions. Splitting the
  spec-drift effort across two of the three v0.2 features risks
  serialising what could otherwise run in parallel.

## Decision

**Option B with Option-C-style upstream recommendations.**

`pilot-v0.2` proceeds within the existing breakout-note v0.2
envelope. The cd-rules pin (`ee01c80...`) and the breakout-note
pin (`3b7b18af...`) both stay frozen unless a downstream
breakout-note revision lands in `Deep-Relaxation-Ordering/diamonds_in_water`
during the v0.2 cycle, in which case Phase 15 picks up the new pin.

For each of the three additions, the implementation defaults
exactly reproduce v0.1 arithmetic:

| Addition | Default that reproduces v0.1 |
|---|---|
| Rayleigh gate | `delta_T_assumed = 0.0 K` (or any value with no convection flag set; v0.1 cache adds the column with `convection_flag = False` everywhere) |
| Radius split | `delta_shell = 0.0` ⇒ `r_hydro ≡ r_material` |
| Polydispersity smearing | `σ_geom → 0` ⇒ delta-function ⇒ sharp v0.1 labels |

Concurrently, three explicit upstream recommendations are recorded
here for the next breakout-note revision:

1. **Convection** *should* be promoted to a §5.1 normative
   inclusion. Convection-dominated cells fall outside the §5.1
   classification envelope (the classifier assumes 1-D vertical
   transport); the spec needs to acknowledge this. The
   recommendation is for v0.3 of the breakout note to absorb a
   short §5.1 paragraph and a `convection_flag` channel in the §6
   deliverable list.
2. **`δ_shell`** stays implementation-level at this pilot's v0.2.
   Promote to a §3 first-class parameter if and when experimental
   functionalisation data motivate it; until then, audit-gap pin in
   §3 is sufficient.
3. **`σ_geom`** stays deliverable-side at this pilot's v0.2.
   Promotion to a §5 first-class axis would 5–10× the cache walk
   cost without proportionate scientific yield; the post-processing
   form of polydispersity smearing is the right level of formality
   for a v0.2 numerical pilot.

These three recommendations are ADR outputs, not Phase 10 commits
to upstream changes. Whether the breakout note adopts them is
sibling-repo work owned by the breakout note's authors.

## Consequences

### Positive

- `pilot-v0.2` opens immediately. No upstream blocking.
- v0.1 cache, deliverables, and tag remain valid and unchanged.
  Bit-for-bit reproducibility of v0.1 outputs is the v0.2 contract.
- The forward-compatibility constraint is enforced at the test
  level (Phase 12.1 reruns the entire v0.1 suite at default
  `δ_shell = 0`, expecting zero diffs). A regression that breaks
  v0.1 outputs is caught at CI time, not at code-review time.
- Three explicit upstream recommendations are now on record for
  the breakout-note authors, ready to be acted on at their tempo.

### Negative

- The breakout note's §3 / §5 sections do not yet carry the
  `δ_shell`, `σ_geom`, or convection terminology. A reader who
  reads only the spec — not the audit-gap footnotes or the
  implementation — may not realise the three knobs exist. This is
  the same kind of "audit gap" the Phase-2.5 lab note already
  flagged for `T_OBS_S` and the 5th depth value; the failure mode
  is the same and the mitigation is the same (explicit footnote
  entry plus this ADR plus a deliverable-index "Known caveats"
  bullet).
- If the breakout note's v0.3 revision absorbs convection or
  `σ_geom` as §5 axes, `pilot-v0.2`'s implementation may need to
  be reorganised. The forward-compatibility constraint limits
  the surface area exposed; rearrangement should be local to
  `regime_map.py` and the new `convection.py` / `polydispersity.py`
  modules.

### Neutral

- The three audit-gap footnotes proposed for upstream are
  text-only entries. They do not change the spec's normative
  content; they just make the `pilot-v0.2` extensions visible
  in the spec index.
- The `pilot-v0.1` tag at `9a0fc76` remains the canonical "feature
  complete against breakout-note v0.2" reference. `pilot-v0.2`
  will tag against the same breakout-note pin unless v0.3 lands
  during the v0.2 cycle.
