Topic

Fluorescent nanodiamonds make exquisite cellular sensors — but only if they are still in suspension.

Nitrogen-vacancy centres in nanodiamond read out local temperature, magnetic field, and pH from inside biological samples. None of that physics is interesting if the particles in the cuvette have already settled to the bottom by the time the camera captures the first frame.

This repository is the simulation that answers the prior question. Given a particle radius, sample temperature, cell depth, and observation time, it classifies a suspension as homogeneous, stratified, or sedimented; reports continuous boundary radii via root-finding; emits a fidelity envelope; and ships a design table an experimentalist can read directly.

The implementation is a numerical pilot in the literal sense: three independent solvers (analytical, Langevin ensemble, Smoluchowski PDE) cross-validate one another across a 30 × 7 × 5 × 6 parameter sweep. Every conclusion below is grounded in that sweep.

Long horizon

The pilot is the first of three layers; each closes one scientific question and earns one major release.

Each layer is gated by an experimental campaign — not an internal "looks right" check. The simulator is the platform; the science sits on top. Full chart in program-context.md.

Findings

Five conclusions follow directly from the §5 sweep.

Each is grounded in the 6300-cell cache committed at notebooks/data/regime_map_grid.csv. Full narrative with regime tables and anchor-cell case studies in findings — physics.

Headline

Across the full sweep, 45 % of cells are sedimented and only 18 % stay homogeneous. By unique experimental cell, 57 % sediment at least once within a week and only 13 % remain homogeneous at every observation time. The diamond–water system is gravity-dominated almost everywhere in the spec's parameter envelope.

Depth dominates

The largest stay-mixed radius scales analytically as r ∝ h-1/3. A 100× change in cell depth shifts the homogeneous edge by exactly the analytic 4.64× ratio. Halving the cuvette is worth more than halving the particle.

Temperature is sub-bin

Across 5 °C → 35 °C the homogeneous edge moves by ~3.4 %; the §5 r-axis (~10 % bin spacing) cannot resolve it. Holding cell depth constant matters more than holding temperature constant.

Time matters more than temperature

At fixed depth and temperature, the sedimented column more than doubles between 1 min and 1 h. Long-time distributions converge to the analytic equilibrium ratio; short-time distributions drift toward it.

Two-thirds is closed-form

67 % of §5 cells are decided by the homogeneous-corner short-circuit, the equilibrated-corner short-circuit, or the asymptotic-sedimentation fallback. Only the remaining 33 % needs the Smoluchowski finite-volume solver, and that one-third sets the wall time.

Round-4 second criterion

The §5.1 sedimented rule is conjunctive: c(h)/c(0) ≤ 0.05 and ∫00.05·h c dz ≥ 0.95. The second condition prevents finite-time profiles in transit from being mis-labelled as sedimented.

Convection caveat

For cells with h ≥ 1 mm, even a 0.1 K vertical gradient can put the suspension into a Rayleigh-unstable regime, breaking the 1-D transport assumption. The simulator flags it; the experimentalist owns the choice.

Practical bracket

Implications

What the L1 result enables for the surrounding programme.

The L1 layer matters even before L2 thermometry and L3 nucleation enter the picture, because every downstream measurement inherits the suspension state at the moment of observation.

  • For NV thermometry experiments. A Brownian-motion measurement only makes sense in cells the simulator labels homogeneous or stratified at the chosen tobs. Sedimented cells are stationary by the time the camera captures the first frame; the apparent temperature read from track diffusivity is then a measurement of the wall, not the bulk fluid.
  • For nucleation studies. Diamond surfaces as heterogeneous nucleation seeds need particles distributed through the cell at t = 0 of the cooling protocol — not concentrated against the bottom. The L3 question is bracketed by L1: the regime map tells you which (radius, depth, cooling time) combinations even have a meaningful nucleation experiment to run.
  • For the wider programme. The simulator's job is to encode the physics literature already has, flag where measurement and theory disagree, and ground uncertainty bounds. It is not a replacement for measurements — a v3.0 result that disagrees with a careful experiment is a model failure, not a measurement failure. See programme-level falsification.
